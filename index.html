Impact of Generative AI Awareness on Education and Economic Disparities

This preliminary analysis, prepared by Scott Glasgow (FinMango) for Chen Zhang, PhD (Iowa State University), examines the potential impact of awareness of Generative AI technologies and tools on bridging educational gaps and alleviating economic disparities, based on Google Trends search data analysis.

Key Observations

  1. Generative AI awareness: Significant increase in search interest for "Generative AI" starting from late 2022.
  2. Specific Generative AI tools: Explosive growth in searches for ChatGPT, with increased interest in DALL-E and Midjourney as well.
  3. AI skills and education: Substantial increase in searches for "AI skills" and modest increases for "Online AI certificates" and "AI education resources."
  4. Economic indicators: No clear trend in "Income inequality" searches, but increased searches for "Economic opportunity."
  5. Educational aspects: Spike in "Online learning" searches during the COVID-19 pandemic, and increased searches for "AI in education."

Analysis

  • Strong positive correlation between Generative AI awareness and interest in AI-related education and skills.
  • Moderate correlation with economic opportunity and AI job creation, suggesting potential for new opportunities.
  • Weak correlation with general online learning, indicating a specific focus on AI-related education.
  • Little to no correlation with income inequality and wealth gap, suggesting that the impact on overall economic disparities may be limited or delayed.

Limitations and Further Research

  • Correlation does not imply causation; these relationships need further investigation.
  • Search data alone cannot provide a complete picture of actual skill acquisition, job market changes, or economic impacts.
  • The time frame for this analysis is relatively short, and long-term trends may differ.
  • Further research should include:
    • Data on actual AI skill acquisition rates and their impact on employment and income.
    • Analysis of demographics accessing AI-related educational resources.
    • More granual data and compare to more ground truth data sources.

Conclusion

The correlation analysis suggests a strong relationship between increased awareness of Generative AI and interest in AI-related education and skills. However, the impact on broader economic disparities appears to be limited in the short term. Further research with additional data sources and methodologies is needed to fully understand the long-term implications of Generative AI awareness on educational gaps and economic disparities.
